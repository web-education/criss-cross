A new dimension

The popularity of CSS grids have been increasing steadily over the past years. They bring balance to our pages, and help us build interfaces more quickly. Most grids are one-dimensional: you specify their width and they adapt in height to their content.

Crisscross, however, is two-dimensional: you can specify both width and height, relative to the width of the container.

Those are the usual grid cells. They will adapt to the height of the content.

Adding a height-* block inside one of those will also extend it.

How to use

Classic grid

The class will start a new containing cells. Every must specify a width ranging from to .

		
  <div class="row">
	  <div class="two cell">Two cell</div>
  	  <div class="ten cell">Ten cell</div>
  </div>

Two dimensional grid

Adding a height, ranging from to , will give the container a height depending on the container's width. This means that a block is a square.

The content inside a height-* block must be wrapped in a absolute cell block.

		
  <div class="row">
	  <div class="two cell height-two">
		  <div class="absolute cell">Two cell height-two</div>
	  </div>
	  <div class="ten cell height-two">
		  <div class="absolute cell">Ten cell height-two</div>
	  </div>
  </div>

Mixed lines

When blocks width add up to more than twelve, the last block falls under its predecessor. 
		
  <div class="row">
	  <div class="two cell height-two">
		  <div class="absolute cell">Two cell height-two</div>
	  </div>
	  <div class="ten cell height-one">
		  <div class="absolute cell">Ten cell height-one</div>
	  </div>
	  <div class="ten cell height-one">
		  <div class="absolute cell">Ten cell height-one</div>
	  </div>
  </div>

Known issues

Sadly, adding a padding to a height-* block will break it. You need to set the padding to a wrapper, to the cell inside your block, or work with margins.
This will break: 
		
  <div class="row">
	  <div class="twelve cell height-two" style="padding:15px">
		  <div class="absolute cell">Twelve cell height-two</div>
	  </div>
  </div>
Twelve cell height-two with 15px padding
This will work: 
		
  <div class="row">
	  <div class="twelve cell height-two">
		  <div class="absolute cell" style="padding:15px">Twelve cell height-two, 15px padding in absolute cell</div>
	  </div>
  </div>
Twelve cell height-two, 15px padding in absolute cell
This will work as well: 
		
  <div class="row">
        <div class="twelve cell" style="padding:15px;background:#333;">
	      <div class="twelve cell height-two">
		      <div class="absolute cell">Twelve cell height-two, 15px padding in a wrapper cell</div>
	      </div>
        </div>
  </div>
Twelve cell height-two, 15px padding in a wrapper cell
